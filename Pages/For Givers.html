The point is this: whoever sows sparingly will also reap sparingly, and whoever sows bountifully will also reap bountifully. Each one must give as he has decided in his hear, not reluctantly or under compulsion, for God loves a cheerful giver.
  2 Corinthians 9:6-7

When you give to someone in need, you submit yourself to Jesus' character over your own. Additionally, you are sharing in the missionary work of the individual you supported. Let's say the missionary you donate to goes onto to show Christ to 5 individuals who accept Jesus as their savior, we believe that you played a role in bringing those individuals to Christ and therefor, have built up reward in Heaven for your obedience.

Let's face it, giving graciously can often seem counter-culture in this day in age. Sure, there are amazing websites and programs out there where individuals can go and give to others. The reason why giving felt counter-cultural to me and could feel that way to you is because of how media portrays self-love and happiness that can only be found in consumption. Think about it, how often do commercials, songs, and other forms of media hint (or outright say) that you would be happier and more fulfilled if you just had more.

- Addison C.
